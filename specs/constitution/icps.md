# Ideal Customer Profiles

> Part of the Mesh Product Constitution
> **Tier:** Extended — adopted to give the founder side of the marketplace a "company-shape" companion to the Aamir persona. The user (layperson) side does not have an ICP — that side is consumer and is fully captured by the Maya persona.

**Last amended:** 2026-04-27

---

## ICP: Early-Stage Consumer / Prosumer AI Founder

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company Size** | 1–3 people (solo founder, co-founder pair, occasionally a 3-person team). Pre-Series-A; bootstrapped or pre-seed. |
| **Industry** | Consumer or prosumer AI tooling — productivity, creative, design, ops, content, education, personal finance, lifestyle. *Not* enterprise SaaS, *not* pure dev-tooling/infrastructure, *not* API-only B2B. |
| **Tech Maturity** | Early adopter / mainstream — ships on a modern stack, deploys via Vercel / Fly / Railway, comfortable with Stripe + standard analytics. |
| **Annual Budget** | $200–$2,000/mo total marketing spend; willing to commit $20–$200 per launch on CPA-priced engagement. |
| **Buying Trigger** | (a) Has shipped publicly within the last 6 months AND first launch (PH / X / HN / Reddit) underperformed expectations, OR (b) has a working product but hasn't launched because they don't trust existing platforms. |
| **Decision Maker** | The founder themselves. |
| **Champion** | The founder themselves — no separate internal champion needed; they are the champion. |

### Qualification Criteria

Must have:

- A working, deployed product (not idea-stage, not waitlist) — Mesh requires a working `product_url` for launch verification.
- A target audience composed of laypeople (per the Maya persona) rather than other engineers, builders, or enterprise buyers.
- A 1-line problem statement and articulable ICP they can write down without flailing.
- Willingness to engage personally with first users — concierge intros are a relationship, not fire-and-forget broadcast.

### Disqualifiers

- **Pre-product** — no MVP shipped. Mesh is not a waitlist platform; come back when launched.
- **Enterprise-only product** — procurement-driven sale, no end-user evaluation.
- **Pure developer-tooling / infrastructure / API-only** — audience is engineers, not the laypeople Mesh's user side serves.
- **Failed verification** — spammy, scammy, deceptive, or the founder cannot substantiate product claims.
- **Founder-of-a-founder-platform** — meta, structurally gameable, wrong category.
- **Distribution-only mindset** — founder seeking reach with no interest in user feedback. The concierge surfaces "skip this" reactions; founders who can't process honest signal will burn the relationship and the brand.
