# Iter-9 raw: Causal / counterfactual / uplift modeling for AI-tool ranking

**Frame.** Iters 2–8 produce match quality. They cannot answer "would recommending Tool X *change* this user's behavior vs. doing nothing / vs. their incumbent tool?" That is uplift τ(u,t) = P(adopt | rec X) − P(adopt | not rec X). Match score is propensity-flavored; uplift is the causal quantity that should drive the final rerank.

## Papers

1. **Wager & Athey, *Estimation and Inference of HTE using Random Forests* (2015/2018).** Foundational: causal forests give pointwise-consistent, asymptotically Gaussian τ(x) estimates with valid CIs — the first defensible "trust interval" on per-user uplift, which matters when Mesh has dozens, not millions, of logged outcomes. [arxiv.org/abs/1510.04342](https://arxiv.org/abs/1510.04342)
2. **Künzel et al., *Meta-learners for HTE (S/T/X-learner)* (2017/2019).** The canonical framing: any supervised learner can be wrapped into a CATE estimator; **X-learner is provably efficient when one arm is much smaller than the other** — exactly Mesh's regime where most (u,t) pairs are never recommended. [arxiv.org/abs/1706.03461](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03461)
3. **Shi, Blei, Veitch, *DragonNet / TARNet adaptations* (2019).** Shared-representation neural HTE with a propensity head + targeted regularization; the natural plug-in over iter-7 (user_embed, tool_embed) since we already produce dense vectors. More data-efficient than naive T-learners on finite logs. [arxiv.org/abs/1906.02120](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.02120)
4. **Swaminathan & Joachims, *Counterfactual Risk Minimization (POEM)* (2015).** Off-policy learning from propensity-logged bandit feedback with variance-regularized IPS — the formal recipe for "given logs of (rec, outcome, propensity), train a better policy." Mesh's ranker becomes a stochastic policy; we log propensities deliberately so this works. [arxiv.org/abs/1502.02362](https://arxiv.org/abs/1502.02362)
5. **Joachims, Swaminathan, de Rijke, *BanditNet* (2018).** Deep-net version of CRM with an equivariant (Self-Normalized-IPS-style) estimator that fixes propensity-overfitting; tells us a neural reranker can be trained directly on logged (rec, click/adopt) without fresh A/B tests. [cs.cornell.edu/people/tj/banditnet/](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/people/tj/banditnet/)
6. **Gao, Zheng, Wang, Feng, He, Li, *Causal Inference in Recommender Systems: Survey* (2022/2023).** The map of the field: confounding, exposure bias, counterfactual data augmentation, uplift-as-objective. Useful as the taxonomy we anchor V2 against. [arxiv.org/abs/2208.12397](https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.12397)
7. **Raja & Vats, *CRM with IPS-Weighted BPR + SNIPS Evaluation* (2025).** Recent, applied: pairs IPS-weighted BPR with a propensity regularizer and SNIPS for offline eval; demonstrates that variance, not bias, is the dominant failure mode of OPE on sparse recsys logs — directly relevant when Mesh has tens of adoptions per tool. [arxiv.org/abs/2509.00333](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.00333)
8. **Jeon et al., *Epinet for Content Cold Start* (2024, Meta/Facebook Reels).** Scalable uncertainty heads on top of a neural recommender to do approximate Thompson sampling — the production blueprint for *generating* the logged data uplift models need, without paying the regret of uniform-random exploration. [arxiv.org/abs/2412.04484](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.04484)

## Synthesis: how this lands in Mesh

**Pipeline target (V2/V3, not V1):** keep iters 2–8 as the *candidate generator*. Add a uplift head τ̂(u,t) on top of (user_embed, tool_embed) trained per Künzel X-learner or DragonNet; rerank the agent's enumerated candidates by τ̂ with a SNIPS-style objective (Raja & Vats). When the per-(u,t) confidence interval (Wager-Athey style, or epinet posterior) is wide, **abstain or fall back to match-score rank** — conformal-flavored selective prediction.

**New depth.** Moves Mesh from "this tool fits your profile" → "this tool will *change what you do tomorrow*". Handles two noises iters 2–8 can't: (a) confounding between match and habit (popular tools look great because everyone clicks; uplift discounts that), (b) substitution — a perfect-match tool the user already owns has zero τ.

**The honest gap — Mesh's bootstrap problem.** Uplift requires logged (recommendation, outcome, propensity) tuples. At <100 users, we have effectively zero. Worse, observational adoption logs without randomization confound recommendation with self-selection; CRM/IPS only buys consistency *if* propensities were logged from a stochastic policy. So uplift is **structurally V2/V3**: V1 must (i) make the ranker stochastic, (ii) log the propensity of every served slot, (iii) accept that the first τ̂ model is months and ~10⁴ events away. There's no math trick around this — it's the central limit theorem.

## Suggested next direction (one)

We are saturating on retrieval depth (8 stages already). The marginal iteration should not be another scoring head — it should be the **bootstrap mechanism that makes every later causal iteration possible**: an **epinet-style Thompson-sampling exploration layer** wrapped around the iter-8 agent's candidate set. Concretely: top-K from agent → epinet posterior over τ̂ (initialized from a weak prior on (user_embed, tool_embed) similarity) → Thompson-sample one or two slots out of every K served, log propensity. This (a) generates the propensity-logged dataset that DragonNet / X-learner / CRM all require, (b) gives V1 users a defensible exploration story ("we sometimes show you something unproven because that's how we learn"), (c) under-cuts the cold-start problem with a principled regret bound rather than uniform random. **It is the smallest iteration that unlocks every causal iteration after it.** If that proves too risky for V1's <100 users, the alternative is a *composition strategy* doc: V1 = iters 2–6 (deterministic match), V1.5 = iter 7+8 (agent + symbolic), V2 = epinet exploration + uplift τ̂ rerank — gated on hitting ~5k logged adoptions.
