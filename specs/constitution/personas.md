# User Personas

> Part of the Mesh Product Constitution
> **Tier:** Extended — adopted because Mesh is a two-sided platform with distinct user types (laypeople and founders) whose needs differ structurally.

**Last amended:** 2026-04-27

---

## Persona: Maya — the tool-drowning layperson

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Non-founder professional or operator (e.g., marketing ops, PM, designer, ops manager, content creator, freelancer). Uses AI tools as part of daily work alongside their primary software stack. Not an engineer, not a builder. May also use AI tools personally. |
| **Goals** | Find AI tools that genuinely save time on specific recurring frictions in their actual workflow. Avoid wasting attention on hyped tools that don't fit. Not feel left behind by the AI wave, but also not chase every shiny new thing. |
| **Pain Points** | (1) 8,000+ AI tools, no way to filter. (2) "Best AI tools for X" articles are commodity SEO that don't know her specific stack or workflow. (3) Friends and Reddit recommend without context. (4) FOMO-driven adoption → bouncing through tools without sticking. (5) Real recurring frictions stay unsolved because the tool that would fix them is unfindable. |
| **Current Workflow** | Hears about a tool on X / Reddit / from a colleague → checks landing page → maybe signs up → tries once or twice → forgets about it. Or: searches "best AI tool for [task]" → gets 8 commodity SEO articles → picks the most-mentioned → same outcome. |
| **Success Metric** | (For Mesh): returns to the concierge unprompted ≥1×/week; tries ≥30% of recommendations within 2 weeks; can name 1–2 tools she's adopted as "essential" because of Mesh. |
| **Technical Comfort** | Medium — uses 5–15 SaaS tools regularly, can configure most products without help, reads release notes, but isn't an engineer. |
| **Frequency of Use** | Weekly — the concierge is designed as a weekly ritual, not daily. |

### Scenarios

1. **Maya (marketing ops, 50-person startup)** opens Mesh Tuesday morning. The concierge says: *"You mentioned your weekly campaign reporting takes 3 hours pulling data from 4 sources. A team launched something this week that auto-pulls Mixpanel/HubSpot/Stripe into a single brief — $20/mo. Worth a 15-min trial?"* She taps **tell me more**, tries the tool, adopts it.
2. **Sam (freelance designer)** gets a *"skip this"* recommendation on a hyped Figma plugin: *"Based on your stack [Figma + Notion + Linear], this doesn't actually save you time vs your existing Linear-template setup. Wait for v2 if you're curious."* He appreciates the honesty, comes back the next week.

## Persona: Aamir — the early-stage consumer AI founder

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Solo or 2-person founder of a consumer or prosumer AI product. Post-MVP, pre-100-paying-users, 0–12 months into the product. Has a working product but no acquisition channel that converts. |
| **Goals** | Get the first 100 real users (not other founders) for the product. Learn what real users actually want from the product (not what other makers think). Stop wasting cycles on launch platforms that don't convert. |
| **Pain Points** | (1) Product Hunt launches: ~0.25% conversion (BrandingStudio.ai = 400 PH signups → 1 paying customer). (2) X "build in public": ~12 signups per 150K impressions over 6 weeks. (3) Reddit niche subs: bans, slow grind, hostile to anything that smells promotional. (4) Newsletter sponsorships: $500+ minimums with no targeting. (5) Cold outbound: spammy, low conversion, expensive in time. (6) Result: weeks of "launching" with no real user, demoralization, pivoting based on noise. |
| **Current Workflow** | Build → post on PH → write a tweet thread → 48-hour spike of vanity metrics → silence → start over with the next launch. |
| **Success Metric** | (For Mesh): ≥30 qualified engagements (click + dwell + downstream action) within 14 days of launch; ≥3 of those convert to paying users; CPA below other paid channels they've tried. |
| **Technical Comfort** | High — engineer-founder or design-founder; configures UTMs, reads analytics, works with API integrations. |
| **Frequency of Use** | Per-launch, not daily. Expected cadence: a launch every 2–6 weeks if Mesh works. |

### Scenarios

1. **Aamir ships a Notion→Linear sync tool.** Submits launch to Mesh: lightweight verification (product URL, ICP, problem statement, X profile) approves within 24h. Mesh suggests communities (r/marketing-ops, r/product-managers, r/notion-power-users) and the concierge identifies 47 user profiles whose stated workflow includes Notion→Linear copying as a recurring friction. Within 7 days: 12 of 47 tap *tell me more*, 8 try the product, 4 convert to paying users. CPA is lower than any other channel he's tried.
2. **Tara launches a Calendly-replacement for AI-tool meetings.** Profile-match returns only 5 users — niche too narrow to drive revenue, but those 5 give her direct "this is what's broken" feedback in concierge chat. The "failed" launch becomes the most valuable user research she's done. She pivots positioning before her next launch.
